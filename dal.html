Dinner — Option 2

One-Pot Red Lentil & Spinach Dal with Seared Tofu

3 servings ~582 cal ~34g protein ~15.4g fiber

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried red lentils
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes, canned
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1.5 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 0.5 tsp turmeric
  • 1 cup light coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp ghee or coconut oil
  • 1 cup cooked basmati rice
  • 10 oz firm tofu, cubed (about 3/4 of a standard 14 oz block)

Steps

  1. Press tofu between paper towels for 5-10 minutes to remove excess water, then cube.
  2. Heat half the ghee in a large pot. Sauté onion, garlic, and ginger for 3-4 minutes.
  3. Stir in curry powder, cumin, and turmeric and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add lentils, diced tomatoes, coconut milk, and 1.5 cups water. Bring to a boil, then simmer uncovered until lentils are soft and the dal thickens, about 15 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, heat the remaining ghee in a skillet and pan-sear the tofu cubes until golden on a couple sides, about 6-8 minutes.
  6. Stir spinach into the dal until wilted. Season to taste.
  7. Serve dal over rice, topped with seared tofu.
Toddler tip: dal is naturally mild and soft-textured, and tofu cubes are easy for little hands to eat.
